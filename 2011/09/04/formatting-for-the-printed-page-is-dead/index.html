Formatting for the printed page is dead

Sun 04 September 2011
By mr.rcollins

In blog.

tags: mobile

The PrintShop

In the mid 80's it seemed like the second most used program for the Apple II was Broderbund's The PrintShop, right after that pirated copy of AppleWorks. For the first time, users could create posters, cards, etc. all with fancy fonts and clip arts. As word processors and page layout tools became more and more commonplace, the final product to be shared was an 8 1/2" x 11" (or A4 :-)) sized sheet of paper.

The world is going mobile

The signs are all around us that the personal computer as we know it is dying, to replaced with mobile technologies. Whenever I do PD, especially with iOS devices or other portable devices, I tell the participants that in 5 years everyone will be using portable devices instead of computers. Unfortunately, this doesn't go over very well. The most common concern is how will they word process. After thinking about it, I realized what was wrong with this question. In the future you will not be creating content that the final product will be the printed page. You, or your students, will be creating content that will be consumed on other mobile devices. The concept of the printed page is dying.
